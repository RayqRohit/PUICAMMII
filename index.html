About Parul University

With a legacy of over three decades in higher education, Parul University has emerged as one of India's leading institutions fostering innovation, advancing research, supporting industry engagement, and strengthening internationalisation. Designated as a Centre of Excellence by the Government of Gujarat and ranked among the top 1.8% universities in India with a NAAC A++ accreditation, the University stands as an epitome of academic and research excellence.

Parul University is ranked among the Top 50 Institutions in India for Innovation (NIRF 2025) and among the Top 150 Universities in India. Home to more than 65,000 students, including 5,500+ international students from 75 nationalities, the University offers a truly global academic environment.

Aligned fully with NEP 2020, Parul University offers 250+ multidisciplinary programmes across leading faculties. Spread across a 150-acre eco-friendly campus, the University houses state-of-the-art facilities including micro-nano technology laboratory, NABL-accredited environmental laboratory, AICTE-supported Idea and Drone Labs, Centre for Future Skills, and a 1000-bedded teaching hospital.

Recognising its progressive milestones, Parul University has been awarded a Diamond Rating in QS I-Gauge 2025.

The Faculty of Applied Sciences nurtures scientific curiosity, research orientation, and problem-solving skills essential for addressing real-world challenges. About Department of Life Sciences

The Department of Life Sciences is a multidisciplinary academic unit dedicated to excellence in education, research, and innovation. It encompasses seven major sub-disciplines: Microbiology, Biotechnology, Biochemistry, Genetics, Bioinformatics, Biopharmaceuticals, and Biofarming, addressing critical aspects of contemporary life science research and applications.

The department offers an integrated curriculum combining strong theoretical foundations with extensive laboratory and computational training, enabling students to understand biological systems from molecular to ecosystem levels. Strong collaborations with research-driven industries, biotechnology firms, and pharmaceutical companies facilitate internships, industrial training, collaborative research, and expert interactions. Supported by well-equipped laboratories, modern instrumentation, and experienced faculty engaged in cutting-edge research, the department emphasises skill development, ethical scientific practices, innovation, and interdisciplinary learning. Graduates are well prepared for careers in research and development, healthcare, pharmaceuticals, agriculture, bioinformatics, regulatory affairs, and higher education.

About of the Conference

The 2nd International Conference on Advances in Medical Microbiology (PUICAMM–II) is highly relevant in the current global scenario marked by emerging and re-emerging infectious diseases, increasing antimicrobial resistance, and the growing demand for rapid and accurate diagnostics. Medical microbiology represents a critical thrust area for pharmaceutical, biotechnology, diagnostics, vaccine manufacturing, and public health sectors. The conference aims to create a vital interface between academia, clinicians, industry experts, and policymakers, facilitating the translation of laboratory research into clinically and commercially viable solutions.

The programme also supports capacity building by exposing students and early-career researchers to industry trends, regulatory standards, Good Laboratory Practices (GLP), and translational research opportunities, thereby enhancing employability and research readiness.

▶ Objectives of the Conference ◀

1

To provide an international platform for academicians, researchers, clinicians, and industry professionals to share recent advancements and innovations in medical microbiology.

2

To discuss emerging challenges in infectious diseases, antimicrobial resistance, and public health, and explore novel diagnostic, preventive, and therapeutic strategies.

3

To promote interdisciplinary and collaborative research among academia, healthcare institutions, research organisations, and pharmaceutical and biotechnology industries.

4

To enhance the technical competence and research skills of students, research scholars, and early-career professionals.

5

To encourage translation of basic research into clinical and industrial applications that improve healthcare outcomes and global health preparedness.
